
QUALITATIVE ANALYSIS
======================

1. REALISM OF GENERATED NAMES
------------------------------
All three models learn the character-level patterns of Indian names:
  - Common prefixes: Arj-, Dev-, Raj-, Vik-, Lak-, Pra-
  - Common suffixes: -an, -av, -esh, -ish, -kar, -kumar, -la, -ya
  - Typical name lengths: 4–10 characters

Vanilla RNN produces mostly plausible names but can get stuck in
repetitive patterns (e.g., Rararar) due to vanishing gradients.

BiLSTM generates smoother, more varied names because the gating
mechanism (input/forget/output gates) retains longer-range dependencies.
Bidirectionality helps it learn common prefix-suffix co-occurrence patterns.

RNN+Attention often produces the most culturally authentic-sounding names.
The attention mechanism allows the decoder to "look back" at its generated
history, reducing incoherent character sequences.

2. COMMON FAILURE MODES
------------------------
  - Very short names (1–2 chars): model predicts EOS too early.
  - Very long names (>12 chars): model fails to predict EOS.
  - Phonetically awkward combos: 'Axz', 'Rtk' (unlikely consonant clusters).
  - Over-representation of common names from training data.

3. COMPARISON ACROSS MODELS
----------------------------
  BiLSTM > RNN+Attention > Vanilla RNN on both Novelty and Diversity
  in most runs due to:
  - Better gradient flow (LSTM gates)
  - Richer character context (bidirectional encoding)
  - More stable training (less prone to exploding/vanishing gradients)
